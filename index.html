Computational & Medical Physics

Jose Huacachi Bazán

Modeling how tissue repairs radiation damage — one alpha particle at a time.

I'm a physics student at UTEC (Peru) building multiscale Monte Carlo models that couple radiation transport with agent-based cellular simulation, to understand how DNA damage from low-dose-rate alpha-emitting radionuclides like Actinium-225 gets repaired.

View research Download CV

Now

Writing the theoretical framework chapter of my thesis — currently deep in the Ac-225 decay chain: Bateman equations, secular equilibrium, and branching fractions, cross-checked numerically in Python.

Focus areas

01 — Transport

Radiation Transport

Monte Carlo simulation of alpha-particle transport and energy deposition with OpenTOPAS and TOPAS-nBio.

02 — Tissue

Multiscale Cellular Modeling

Agent-based tissue simulation with CompuCell3D's Cellular Potts Model, coupled through the TOPAS-Tissue framework.

03 — Repair

DNA Damage & Repair

Characterizing double-strand breaks and DSB clusters to model repair kinetics at low dose rates.
